Zero-Waste Ryan Stadium Proposal

Almost as soon as I arrived on Northwestern's campus my freshman year, I searched for ways to get involved in sustainability. That is why I applied to join Northwestern's Associated Student Government Sustainability committee. I worked extremely hard alongside my fellow sub-committee members on a proposal to make Northwestern's football stadium zero-waste. Collaborating with my group members on a major project such as this was incredible experience. I was also able to gain connections within the administration who can help push this project and future projects forward.

We studied current waste management practices at Ryan Field and Northwestern in general compared to other Big Ten institutions. We suggested the implemenation of monitored waste stations, hand-dryers, sustainable alternatives to food container products, and recycling of plastic film. Additionally, we conducted a cost benefit analysis of the project both initally and as it is sustained in future years.
