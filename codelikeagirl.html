Code Like A Girl Summer Camp

During my junior year of high school I was recognized by the National Center for Women in Technology for my aspirations in computing and was awarded $3,000 to organize and direct my own coding summer camp for elementary school girls.

I spent the entire spring and summer leading up to the camp planning activities using Scratch (a block-based programming language), booking field trips to the California Science Museum, and organizing speaker talks with notable women in STEM such as Joy Montoya (a 20 year software development veteran). In addition to building this temporary company on my own, I led a team of three female employees who acted as teaching assistants, honing my leadership skills in the process.

It was an experience I will never forget.
